## 📋 Project Overview
An interactive Tableau dashboard analyzing death rate trends, geographic distribution, and demographic breakdowns across Malaysia.

## Key Insights
* **Trend:** Observed a peak in the average death rate in 2021 (7.90) before a gradual decline.
* **Demographics:** Higher total deaths recorded in males (454,229) compared to females (340,337).
* **Geography:** W.P. Kuala Lumpur and Petaling identified as the districts with the highest total deaths.

## 🔍 Key Insights & Observations
Based on the visual analysis of the data, several critical trends emerge:

* **Temporal Peak (2021):** There was a significant spike in the average death rate in **2021 (7.90)**, likely correlating with the global pandemic's impact. The rate has since trended downward to **7.07 in 2023**.
* **Demographic Variance:** Male mortality (**454,229**) is notably higher than female mortality (**340,337**), representing a significant gap in total volume.
* **Geographic Hotspots:** **W.P. Kuala Lumpur** and **Petaling** are identified as the top two districts by total deaths, aligning with high population density areas in the Klang Valley.
* **State-Level Distribution:** **Selangor** consistently records the highest total deaths for both genders among all states.

---

## 📈 Key Insights & Findings

Based on the data visualized from 2020 to 2023, the following insights were identified:

### 1. The 2021 Pandemic Peak
* **Trend Analysis:** The Average Death Rate saw a significant spike in **2021**, reaching **7.90**.
* **Recovery:** Following the 2021 peak, there has been a steady decline to **7.07** by 2023, though it remains higher than the 2020 baseline of **6.45**.
* **Total Impact:** The cumulative total deaths recorded over this period stand at **794,566**.

### 2. Gender Mortality Gap
* **The Data:** Male deaths (**454,229**) significantly outnumber female deaths (**340,337**).
* **Finding:** There is a **25% higher mortality volume in males** compared to females across the recorded timeframe.

### 3. Urban Hotspots (District Analysis)
* **Top Districts:** **W.P. Kuala Lumpur**, **Petaling**, and **Johor Bahru** are the top three districts with the highest death counts.
* **Geographic Insight:** Mortality is heavily concentrated in the **Klang Valley** and highly urbanized economic hubs, as seen in the "Top Death by District" bar chart.

### 4. Regional Distribution
* **State Leaders:** While KL leads as a district, **Selangor** (containing Petaling, Ulu Langat, and Klang) shows the highest cumulative mortality across both genders.
* **Geospatial Trend:** The West Coast of Peninsular Malaysia shows a much higher density of mortality "hotspots" compared to East Malaysia.
